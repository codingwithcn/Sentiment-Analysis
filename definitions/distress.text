great pain, anxiety, or sorrow; acute physical or mental suffering; affliction; trouble: distress over his mother's illness.a state of extreme necessity or misfortune: After the stock market crash, he found himself in great financial distress.the state of a ship or airplane requiring immediate assistance, as when on fire in transit.that which causes pain, suffering, trouble, danger, etc.: His willful disobedience was a distress to his parents.liability or exposure to pain, suffering, trouble, etc.; danger: a damsel in distress.Law. the legal seizure and detention of the goods of another as security or satisfaction for debt, etc.; the act of distraining.the thing seized in distraining.
